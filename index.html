Dr. Shirley Ann Jackson is a pioneering physicist who became the first African American woman to earn a PhD from MIT and later served as president of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. Her groundbreaking research in physics and telecommunications enabled the development of technologies such as caller ID and call waiting. As well as being a recipient of the National Medal of Science, Dr. Jackson has also been an influential leader in science policy, serving as chair of the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission.

This website was developed by a team of Indiana University students as their final project in the course INFO I-101 Introduction to Informatics. The team, consisting of Jay Duggal, Youssef Ahmar, and Matthew Ritten, was assigned to pick an underrepresented innovator relating to informatics. The team picked Dr. Shirley Jackson due to her major achievements relating to telecommunications, an aspect that informatics covers through its sociotechnical analysis. We also picked Dr. Jackson due to her extreme underrepresentation within the jungle of technological advancements. The project consisted of researching the innovator, developing a website presenting our research, gaining feedback and critical criticism on the website, and applying said feedback to a final website draft.
